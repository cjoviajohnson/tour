Ernakulam

The district of Ernakulam takes its name from Ernakulam, which is the centre of Kochi in Kerala, India. Here are several significant buildings, including the Kerala High Court, the Kochi Municipal Corporation office, and the Cochin Shipyard. The administrative centre of the Ernakulam District was first located at Ernakulam but was subsequently moved to Kakkanad, an area in eastern Kochi. The former capital of the Kingdom of Cochin was Ernakulam. The area can affirm that during the ancient and mediaeval periods, it significantly aided Kerala's trading contacts with the outside world. The Chera Dynasty of the Sangam era, which reigned over substantial sections of Kerala and Tamil Nadu, is tied to the early political history of Ernakulam. The region was thereafter governed by the Kingdom of Cochin after the Cheras.

Major Attractions

Hill Palace

Hill Palace is an archaeological museum and palace located in the Tripunithura neighbourhood of Kochi city in the Indian state of Kerala. It is the largest archeological museum in the state and was the imperial administrative office and official residence of the Cochin Maharaja. Built in 1865, the palace complex consists of 49 buildings in the traditional architectural style, spreading across 54 acres. The complex has an archaeological museum, a heritage museum, a deer park, a pre-historic park and a children’s park.[1] The campus of the museum is home to several rare species of medicinal plants. Presently the palace has been converted into a museum by The Kerala State Archaeology Department and is open to public.

The National Shrine Basilica of Our Lady of Ransom

The National Shrine Basilica of Our Lady of Ransom, also known as Vallarpadam Basilica, is a minor basilica and a significant Christian pilgrimage site in India. It is situated in Vallarpadam, an area of Ernakulam in the city of Kochi. The Basilica receives almost 5 million visitors each year. It is India's most significant Marian Shrine. [Reference required] People from all around the world, regardless of caste or faith, visit churches to ask the Blessed Virgin Mary, also known as "Vallarpadathamma," for favours.

Ernakulam Shiva Temple

One of Kerala's most important temples, Ernakulam Shiva Temple, also called Ernakulathappan Temple, is situated in the centre of Ernakulam, Kochi, Kerala, India. According to local Hindu beliefs and customs, the temple, which is devoted to Lord Shiva, is regarded as the city temple, with the presiding god serving as the city's guardian. The deity is reverently referred to as Ernakulathappan, which means Lord of Ernakulam, in accordance with typical practise in Kerala. At the moment, Cochin Devaswam Board is in charge of running the temple. Under the active sponsorship of Diwan Sri Edakkunni Sankara Warrier, the temple was constructed in its present shape in 1846, elevating it to the status of a Royal temple in the Kochi Kingdom. The temple is situated on a single acre of ground.
