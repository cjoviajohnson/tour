Kannur

Kannur, also known as Cannanore in English, is a city and a Municipal Corporation in the Indian state of Kerala. Its pronunciation is [kur] (listen). It is the administrative centre for the Kannur district and is located 137 kilometres (85 miles) south of Mangalore and 274 kilometres (170 miles) north of Kochi, a major port city and economic centre. The city of Kannur was once known as Cannanore during the time of British colonial control in India, when it was a part of the Malabar District (Madras Presidency). In Kerala, Kannur is the sixth-largest urban agglomeration. According to the 2011 census, there were 232,486 people living in Kannur Municipal Corporation, the municipal organisation in charge of running the city's mainland region.

Major Attractions

St. Angelo Fort

Three kilometres from Canannore (Kannur), a city in Kerala state, south India, lies St. Angelo Fort, also known as Kannur Fort or Kannur Kotta, a fort that faces the Arabian Sea. Portuguese were given permission to establish a settlement in Kerala in 1498 by the native Kolathiri monarch, who was Vasco da Gama's host during his voyage to India. He authorised Francisco de Almeida, the ruler of Portugal, to construct a fort there on October 23, 1505. The foundation stone was set the very following day, on October 24, 1505, by Goncalo Gil Barbosa, the Portuguese factor of Cannanore (Kannur). On October 30, 1505, the timber fort's construction was finished.

Arakkal Royal Palace

The sole Muslim royal dynasty in Kerala, India, is the Arakkal family, whose museum is named after them. Actually, the museum is a portion of the Arakkalkettu (Arakkal Royal Palace). The Kerala State Government turned the palace's durbar hall part into a museum. It was renovated for Rs. 9,000,000 before being reopened in July 2005.The Arakkalkettu was rebuilt by the government, but it remains privately held by the Arakkal Royal Trust and is not governed by the Archaeological Survey of India, the nation's archaeological agency. The Arakkal Family's tradition, which was important to Malabar's history, was being carefully preserved by the government..

Kannur Lighthouse

In Kerala state, south India, the Kannur Lighthouse is situated close to Payyambalam Beach and a few kilometres from Kannur town. The Sea View Park and the Government Guest House are nearby. The lighthouse, which looks out over the Arabian Sea, is still in operation.The historical English name for the town of Kannur was Cannanore. Even today, the Kannur lighthouse is frequently referred to as the Cannanore lighthouse.
