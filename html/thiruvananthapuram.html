Thiruvananthapuram

The capital of the Indian state of Kerala is Thiruvananthapuram, formerly known as Trivandrum. With a population of 957,730, it is the most populated city in Kerala. The University of Kerala, APJ Abdul Kalam Technological University, the regional office of Indira Gandhi National Open University, as well as several other universities and institutions are located in Thiruvananthapuram, a well-known centre for education and research.

Research facilities like the National Institute for Interdisciplinary Science and Technology and the Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre of the Indian Space Research Organization are also located in Thiruvananthapuram. With the Southern Air Command headquarters of the Indian Air Force, the Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station, and the planned Vizhinjam all located in India's largest metropolis in the deep south, it is strategically significant.

Major Attractions

The Shree Padmanabhaswamy Temple

The Shree Padmanabhaswamy Temple is a Hindu temple located in Thiruvananthapuram, capital of the state Kerala, India. The name of the city of 'Thiruvananthapuram' in Tamil and Malayalam translates to "The City of Lord Ananta", (The City Of Lord Vishnu) referring to the main deity of the temple. The temple is built in an intricate fusion of the Chera style and Dravidian style of architecture, featuring high walls, and a 16th-century gopura.

Shankumugham Beach

Shankumugham Beach is a beach in Thiruvananthapuram district of Kerala, south India. The beach is on the western side of Thiruvananthapuram (Trivandrum) and very near to Trivandrum International Airport.The vast stretch of white sand and the serene atmosphere, away from the crowd in the city, provide all the ingredients for relaxation and for spending an ideal evening. - The city of the Holy Anantha Shanghumugham beach is considered as the ‘Arattukadavu’ of Sri Ananthapadmanabhan - The presiding deity of the city. Thousands of Hindus perform ‘Bali Tharpanam’ during special occasions. Shankhumugham beach is the prime location for Ganesh Nimarjan at the time of Vinayaka Chathurthi. . Boating facilities are offered at this picnic spot. Speed boats, restaurants and well-landscaped gardens are available.

The Napier Museum

The Napier Museum is an art and natural history museum situated in Thiruvananthapuram, India. A building was constructed in 1857 during the reign of Uthram Thirunal Marthanda Varma, and later demolished in 1874 to lay foundation for a new building, during the reign of Ayilyam Thirunal. The Governor of Madras in 1872 appointed Robert Chisholm, a consulting architect of the Madras Government to design a new museum. In 1880, construction finished and the museum opened to the public, bearing the namesake of its governor, Francis Napier, 10th Lord Napier. Some of the building features include a gothic roof and minarets. The Napier Museum, a landmark of the city, is noted for its unique ornamentation and architectural style, which is influenced by Indian, Chinese, Kerala and Mughal Schools of Architecture.
